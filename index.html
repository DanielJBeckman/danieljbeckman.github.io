An embedded computer vision system designed to measure river stage by detecting staff plates in the field. Gage-Cam runs a U-Net model trained to segment the physical shape of a staff plate from imagery, allowing precise calculation of water height.

Cuffee Beach

A stylized VR experience depicting the First Nations burial ground at Cuffee Beach, NY, and visualizing the impact of sea level rise. The project featured an immersive shoreline landscape with animated vegetation, interactive water levels, and ambient sound — designed as both a cultural tribute and a climate change awareness tool.

Bronx River Kiosk

This interactive kiosk was developed as part of a public outreach initiative to highlight ongoing water quality monitoring along the Bronx and Harlem Rivers. Designed for the Bronx Zoo and built as an iOS application for iPad, the exhibit showcases how scientists collect and analyze real-time environmental data. Visitors can explore data from local sensor stations, learn about key indicators like temperature, turbidity, and oxygen levels, and discover how this data helps protect wildlife and public health.
