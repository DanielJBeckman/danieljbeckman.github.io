AR Flood

Projecting water into the real world. An augmented reality tool for simulating flood height and extent using LiDAR, scene understanding, and geospatial input.

What is AR Flood?

AR Flood is a mixed reality visualization tool that lets you see what flooding would look like on-site. Given a geographic coordinate and a predicted flood height, it uses your mobile device’s LiDAR and spatial mapping features to simulate floodwaters in the real world.

By applying a physics-aware shader in real-time, the water visually obeys the shape of the environment—pooling against walls, flowing around terrain, and aligning with actual surfaces. This gives scientists, responders, and the public an intuitive, in-place view of flood risk and impact.

Key Features

  • Uses LiDAR and ARKit scene understanding
  • Simulates realistic floodwater behavior
  • Renders water via Shader Graph in mixed reality
  • Simple coordinate + height input for deployment

Originally developed by the USGS HAIL team, this tool helps bridge the gap between geospatial data and field-based communication.
