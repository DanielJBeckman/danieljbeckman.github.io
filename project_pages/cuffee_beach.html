Cuffee Beach

An immersive VR experience that reconstructs a First Nations burial ground and visualizes sea level rise through stylized simulation, environmental storytelling, and real-time interaction.

Overview

Cuffee Beach VR is a fully realized virtual experience designed to explore the impact of sea level rise on a culturally significant shoreline site in New York. Using an elevation map to generate real terrain in Blender, the entire environment was recreated in stylized 3D — replacing physical objects with digital assets, many of them hand-crafted for historical accuracy and visual fidelity.

Key Features

  • Terrain generated from high-resolution elevation data
  • Custom digital asset replacements for all real-world objects
  • Handcrafted VR rig and in-VR user interface
  • 3D spatial audio and lighting effects for realism and immersion
  • Custom Shader Graph water engine with animated waves and underwater refraction
  • Dynamic sea level rise masks (3ft, 6ft, 9ft) to trigger flood progression
  • Vegetation damage effects triggered by saltwater inundation
  • Integrated animation, atmosphere, and visual storytelling

Purpose

Created as a cultural tribute and climate awareness tool, this project blends technical craft and environmental storytelling to help users engage with the future of our coastal sites in a visceral, emotionally grounded way.
