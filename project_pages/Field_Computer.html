Field Computer

A rugged, self-contained computer vision platform for remote deployments — solar-powered, waterproof, and satellite-connected.

Overview

This fully waterproof field computer was built to support embedded machine learning in remote, power-scarce environments. Designed from scratch, the system integrates a Raspberry Pi, solar charging, onboard power cell, camera module, and both cellular and RockBLOCK satellite telemetry capabilities. It is capable of running real-time PyTorch inference at the edge.

Power System Design

Power System Engineering The field computer was built around an ultra-low-power architecture featuring a through-charging lithium battery paired with a compact 9W solar panel. Power draw was minimized through efficient component selection and strategic system sleep states. A Witty Pi power management board enabled scheduled power cycling, ensuring the device could hibernate and restart autonomously. The system also incorporated a fault-tolerant startup and recovery design, minimizing the need for field maintenance and making it ideal for long-term remote deployment.

Telemetry Options

The telemetry system was originally designed to transmit AI inference results along with a compressed luminance-only image using the RockBLOCK Iridium short-burst data protocol. Images were encoded in small batches suitable for rapid-fire satellite transmission and later reassembled on the receiving end for quality control and verification. While the final build deployed with a cellular modem for ease of testing, the architecture supports both satellite and cellular telemetry for adaptable field operations.

Field Ready

The unit was tested for waterproofing, temperature tolerance, and vibration resistance. Its compact size and rugged housing make it ideal for long-term remote deployment, such as riverside gage stations, mountaintop cameras, or storm-surge monitors.
